# Schedule
- Weekly, Except the Midterms
- 90 mins:
	- 20-40 min presentation
	- 40 min discussion
	- 10 min wrap-up

# Roles
- **Chair** (me): Opens & wraps up the session, manages the papers and the club itself.
- **Glossary Curator** (who?): writes down the important terms, researches on them and puts them on the knowledge hub. *this can change weekly*
- **Schedule Steward**: (who? *could be merged with Chair*): talks to presenters and specifies weekly papers & presenters. Updates and maintains the calendar accordingly. 
- **Session Spotlight**: (changes weekly): has read the paper (or the week's part of the book) in detailed and has prepared a presentation.
# Minute Ideas, *To Be Expanded As We Go*
- A dynamic Google form to gather peer-feedback, then changing the verdict for transparency. Current one could be:
	- How helpful was the glossary?
    - Was the discussion engaging?
    - Was 90 mins reasonable?

- Glossary of a few words each week to which we can refer later. 
- Occasional sessions with researchers if possible
- A summary and an Audio review (NotebookLM) posted ~2 days before each session for those who do not have the time to read the full thing.
- The focus is to read papers, we could read different parts of a book as for prerquisite, though this should not take too long. Also, the goal is not to understand everyhting.
- Arcs (and levels) on different topics
- Paper Reviews based on discussions in the session that can be published on AMCSUI Journal
- In order to make the sessions more useful and serious, this should not be a weekly "drop-in whenever you want" there should be some kind of sign-up process coupled with a some sort of a qualification. This could be in the form of a Google Form and then talking to the person.
